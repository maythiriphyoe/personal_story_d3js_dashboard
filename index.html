Personal well-being & performance engine

📖 About this project

This dashboard was created for the Data Communications class in Spring 2026. It uses 20 days of personal habit and well-being data collected through daily tracking. The dashboard includes data such as sleep hours, screen time, learning hours, productivity, mood, stress, social energy, and journaling habits. The project was built using D3.js.

💡 Key insights

Stress during study periods

Days with longer learning hours often had higher stress levels. Productivity sometimes increased during stressful days, but mood usually became lower afterward.

Social energy drops

Heavy study days were often linked to lower social energy. Spending more time learning sometimes left less energy for social interaction and communication.

Journaling pattern

Journaling appeared more often on stressful days. This suggests journaling was mostly used after stress happened instead of being part of a consistent daily routine.

🎯 What I learned

After tracking my habits for 20 days, I noticed a few patterns that may help improve my routine and well-being:

  • Balance study time: Long study hours often increased stress and reduced social energy. Taking breaks and avoiding long study sprints may help prevent burnout.
  • Journal regularly: Journaling mostly happened on stressful days. Turning it into a regular habit may help manage stress earlier.
  • Make time for hobbies: Creative and enjoyable activities were linked to better mood scores. Scheduling hobby time during busy weeks may help improve emotional well-being.

⚖️ Ethics & limitations

🔒 Privacy

This dashboard only uses general self-tracked data such as mood, stress, sleep, and productivity. Personal journal details were removed and grouped into broad categories like Hobby, Entertainment, and Achievement.

⚠️ Limitations

  • The dataset only covers 20 days, so it may not fully represent long-term patterns.
  • Mood and stress scores were self-reported and may change depending on emotions or memory.
  • Some entries were logged later, so a few details may not be perfectly accurate.

📊 Visualization choices

Simple and color-blind friendly colors were used to improve readability. Different chart types were chosen to make trends and comparisons easier to understand.

🤖 AI use & Additional context

Gemini AI was used to help with D3.js coding, debugging, correlation calculations, and improving the dashboard layout and styling.

The insights in this dashboard are based only on the collected data and may not include other factors such as physical health, diet, or outside events.
