Personal well-being & performance engine

📖 Story behind

This project is meant to build an interactive personal story dashboard for the **Data Communications Class for Spring 2026 semester**. This project is built by **D3.js** and uses the personal behavior data collected for 20 days. The variables include sleep hours, screen time, learning duration, productivity scores, social energy reserves, stress levels, daily highlight categories, and proactive mental health habits (such as journaling).

💡 Key insights

Cognitive cost of sprints

Intensive learning periods act as direct catalysts for emotional distress, exhibiting a high positive correlation (r = 0.59) with stress spikes. While stress manages to temporarily push up productivity scores (r = 0.33), it compromises emotional coping mechanisms, leading directly to compressed mood states (r = -0.40).

Social battery drain

Extended learning intervals systematically exhaust internal social energy structures (r = -0.58). This highlights a structural trade-off where heavy intellectual progress directly shrinks interpersonal and communication capacities for the day.

The reactive routine flaw

Isolating the journaling metric exposes a noticeable structural issue: days featuring active journaling correlate with higher stress baselines (6.0 vs 5.0). This demonstrates that mindfulness habits are currently deployed as an emergency damage control mechanism after stress spikes occur, rather than acting as daily preventive maintenance.

🎯 Data-backed decisions

Based on the data gathered over this 20-day tracking period, several clear behavioral modifications should be implemented to break unhealthy feedback loops:

  • Enforcing a learning threshold: Since learning hours have a sharp negative correlation with social energy (r = -0.58) and drive up stress levels (r = 0.59), study sprints must be capped. When social energy registers below 5, learning allocations should immediately scale down to protect the emotional baseline from crashing.
  • Transitioning from reactive to proactive habits: The data highlights that journaling currently tracks alongside higher stress levels (avg 6.0 vs 5.0). To fix this anomaly, journaling should be relocated to a fixed morning routine, transforming it into an objective, preventive shield rather than an emergency coping tool used only after burnout hits.
  • Strategic restructuring of downtime: Passively treating downtime as an emergency response to fatigue correlates with lower mood drops (`Relaxation` averages an emotional low of 2.0). Conversely, creative anchors yield peak scores (`Hobby` highlights average 8.0). Active, creative recovery should be intentionally scheduled into mid-week blocks rather than waiting for burnout to force passive recovery.

⚖️ Ethics & responsibility

🔒 Privacy statement

Included data: This dashboard logs high-level self-reported psychometric parameters and basic time tracking.
Anonymization method: Raw qualitative text descriptions from personal journals were permanently scrubbed. Emotional outputs and contextual milestones were abstracted into uniform operational definitions and broad categories (e.g., Hobby, Entertainment, Achievement) to eliminate personal details while preserving complete analytical utility.

⚠️ Bias & limitations

  • Memory bias: Compiling retroactive logs carries an inherent risk of historical recency filtering, where past days may be inadvertently scored based on recent emotional states.
  • Small dataset: A 20-day window represents a brief snapshot. It cannot accurately account for long-term seasonal trends, academic cycles, or broader shifts in routine.
  • Subjective scoring: Metrics like mood and stress are logged via self-reported rankings (1–10) without external biometric calibration, introducing variance based on daily mindset.

📊 Visualization justification

Color-blind safe matrix: Traditional red-green color styling was avoided in favor of a purple/orange diverging scheme, clearly distinguishing conflicting correlations. Centered metrics eliminate visual guesswork.
Decoupled scatter tracking: The mood vs. social energy chart explicitly drops screen time noise to focus entirely on social battery depletion thresholds.
Isolated toggles: The wellness profile isolates journaling as a binary field to prevent confounding variables from masking trend interpretations.

🎯 Responsible decision & AI use

Decision limitations: These planned behavioral adjustments assume that observed correlations represent direct, isolated causations. They fail to track omitted variables like changes in physical health, diet, or workplace adjustments that independently affect mood.

Use of AI: **Gemini AI** was utilized in this project to assist with code scaffolding for the D3.js data binds, debug programmatic canvas logic, compute Pearson correlation tables for matrix verification, and polish formatting styles for academic presentation.
